# Propel — Legal Structure Memo

*Decision memo, July 2026. Partner recommendation — verify specifics with a CAC-accredited professional before filing. Costs are estimates.*

## The reality to structure around

Propel is built by **one human owner (Adedamola) + an AI managing partner**. Under Nigerian law only humans/legal persons can hold ownership — an AI cannot be a partner or shareholder. So despite how we work, legally this is a **single-owner business**, which actually simplifies everything:

- A registered *partnership* (2+ persons) is off the table and unnecessary.
- The partnership term sheet (ops/templates/partnership-term-sheet.md) is **DORMANT** — revive it only if a human co-founder or investor ever joins. No equity split, no vesting, no deadlock clauses needed today. That's one less thing to pay a lawyer for.

## The options

| | **Business Name (sole proprietorship)** | **Private Ltd (single shareholder — allowed under CAMA 2020)** |
|---|---|---|
| CAC cost (incl. modest agent fees) | ~₦60k–₦100k | ~₦150k–₦250k+ |
| Speed | Days | 1–3 weeks typical |
| Liability | **Unlimited — personal assets exposed** | Limited to company assets |
| Tax | Personal income tax (PIT, Lagos LIRS); simpler filings | Companies income tax regime; small companies (turnover <₦25m/yr) currently enjoy 0% CIT rate — but more compliance (annual returns, audited accounts as you grow) |
| Credibility with developers/corporates | Adequate for retainers | Materially better for ₦1.5m+ Launch contracts |
| Banking | Business account available | Corporate account, cleaner separation |
| Conversion later | Straightforward: register the Ltd, migrate contracts/bank | — |

## Recommendation: Business Name now, Ltd on triggers

**File a CAC Business Name — "Propel Digital" (fallbacks: Propel Media, Propel Digital Marketing) — this week.** At ₦500k capital, spending a quarter of it on incorporation is bad allocation; ₦60k–₦100k gets you invoicing legally, a business bank account, and Paystack onboarding. Our liability exposure at this stage is modest *if* the MSA is tight (see mitigations).

**Upgrade to a single-shareholder Ltd when ANY of these fires:**
1. First developer **Launch contract ≥₦1.5m** signed or seriously in negotiation (liability + counterparty credibility both jump);
2. **MRR ≥₦1.5m** for 2 consecutive months (the business is now worth protecting);
3. **First employee** hired (PAYE, contracts, separation of obligations);
4. Any client demands it for procurement (some corporates/developers will).

Expected timing per docs/07: trigger 1 or 2 fires around month 3–4 — so budget the Ltd upgrade (~₦250k incl. lawyer) out of *revenue*, already anticipated in the bootstrap plan's "developer swing" proceeds.

## Liability mitigations while sole prop (do all four)

1. **MSA clauses are the armor:** limitation of liability capped at fees paid; explicit "we market, we do not verify title — client warrants all property facts in writing"; indemnity from client for claims arising from their listings. Non-negotiable in every contract from client #1.
2. **Client vetting checklist enforced** (ops/templates/marketing-audit.md) — our biggest real risk is marketing a disputed property; we screen it out at the top of the funnel.
3. **Separate business bank account from day 1** even as sole prop — clean books make the Ltd conversion and any future diligence trivial.
4. **No holding client ad money** (already policy, docs/03) — removes the largest fiduciary exposure an agency has.

## Tax notes (sole prop phase)

- Register TIN with FIRS/JTB; personal income tax to Lagos LIRS on business profits.
- **VAT:** businesses under ₦25m annual turnover are currently exempt from charging VAT — likely us in year 1; confirm current thresholds with the accountant, and revisit pricing language ("VAT where applicable") in docs/03.
- Keep the docs/05 discipline anyway: sweep a tax provision on every receipt; the accountant retainer (₦50k/mo) starts when revenue does, not before.
