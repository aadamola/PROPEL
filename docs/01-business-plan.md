# Propel — Business Plan

*AI-forward digital marketing for real estate professionals in Nigeria.*
*Prepared July 2026 · All figures in Nigerian Naira (₦) · Assumptions stated inline.*

## 1. Executive Summary

**Propel** is a remote-first digital marketing agency built exclusively for the Nigerian real estate industry. We help realtors, estate surveyors & valuers, agents, and small-to-mid developers generate qualified buyer and investor leads through AI-accelerated content, paid media, and marketing systems — at a price point and speed no generalist agency can match.

**The core insight:** Nigerian real estate is sold on trust and relationships, but discovered online. Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and Google are where diaspora buyers and Lagos professionals now start their property search — yet the vast majority of agents and surveyors market with amateur WhatsApp broadcasts, recycled flyers, and no lead capture whatsoever. The few agencies serving this niche are either expensive generalists or one-person freelancers with no systems.

**The wedge:** We productize real estate marketing into fixed-price monthly packages (₦250k–₦1.2m/month) and use AI across the entire delivery chain — listing copy, video scripting and editing, ad creative variants, lead qualification chatbots, CRM automation — so a lean team of 3–5 delivers what traditionally takes an agency of 15. That's our margin engine and our moat.

**The ask (internal):** Partners co-fund ~₦9.5m (~$6,300 at ₦1,500/$) to cover incorporation, tooling, and 6 months of lean runway. Target: break-even by month 6–8 on 8–10 retainer clients; ₦100m+ annualized revenue run-rate by end of year 2.

## 2. Market Analysis

### 2.1 The Nigerian real estate market

- Nigeria's real estate sector is one of the largest contributors to GDP outside oil, with Lagos as its undisputed hub — Lekki, Ajah, Ibeju-Lekki, Ikoyi, Victoria Island, Ikeja, and the emerging Epe corridor are in a sustained construction boom.
- A national housing deficit measured in the tens of millions of units keeps demand structurally high, especially at the ₦20m–₦150m band (off-plan apartments, serviced estates) where marketing wins deals.
- **The diaspora is the whale:** Nigerians abroad remit roughly $20B+ annually, and a meaningful share targets property. Diaspora buyers *cannot* inspect in person first — they buy through content: video walkthroughs, live virtual tours, documentation explainers, and the perceived professionalism of the seller's online presence. This is precisely the product we sell.
- Trust is the binding constraint. "Omonile" land disputes and fraud stories make buyers deeply skeptical. Marketing that signals verification, professionalism, and regulatory standing (NIESV/ESVARBON membership) converts disproportionately well — and licensed surveyors & valuers underuse this credential badly.

### 2.2 The buyer-side behavior shift

- Property discovery has moved to Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube, and Google search; transactions still close on WhatsApp and in person. The winning funnel is therefore: **short-form video → DM/WhatsApp → qualified call → inspection (physical or virtual) → close.**
- Listing portals (PropertyPro, Nigeria Property Centre, BuyLetLive) generate volume but low-intent, unqualified leads. Agents who build their own audience and lead capture consistently out-earn portal-dependent peers.

### 2.3 The customer (our actual buyer)

| Segment | Profile | Pain | Budget reality |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Independent agents/realtors** | 1–5 person operations, commission-driven, Instagram-active but unsystematic | Inconsistent deal flow; no lead capture; content is amateur | ₦150k–₦400k/month if ROI is visible |
| **Estate surveyors & valuers (NIESV/ESVARBON)** | Licensed professionals, often mid-career firms with agency + valuation practice | Losing agency mandates to flashier unlicensed agents; zero digital presence despite superior credentials | ₦250k–₦700k/month; value reputation & compliance-safe marketing |
| **Small/mid developers** | Selling off-plan estates of 10–200 units in Lekki–Epe corridor | Need velocity: units sold per month determines project financing | ₦600k–₦2m+/month per active project; also pay per-project launch fees |
| **Realty brokerages/networks** | 10–100 agent networks (e.g., marketing-cooperative model) | Need brand machinery + agent enablement content at scale | ₦500k–₦1.5m/month |

**Sizing (assumption-based):** Lagos alone has tens of thousands of active agents and 6,000+ registered estate surveyors & valuers nationally, plus hundreds of active estate developments at any time. If our serviceable near-term market is just 2,000 Lagos-based professionals/firms who can pay ≥₦250k/month, a 1% share = 20 clients = ₦60m–₦100m/year. We do not need a large share of this market to build a very profitable firm.

### 2.4 Competition

| Competitor type | Strengths | Weaknesses we exploit |
|---|---|---|
| Generalist digital agencies (Lagos) | Established, full-service | No real estate depth; slow; priced for corporates (₦1.5m+/mo) |
| Freelancers / "social media managers" | Cheap (₦50k–₦150k/mo) | No strategy, no systems, unreliable, no lead accountability |
| Portal ad products (PropertyPro etc.) | Distribution | Rented audience, unqualified leads, no brand equity for the client |
| In-house marketing hires | Control | One mid-level hire costs ₦300k–₦600k/mo + management burden, with narrower skills than our pod |
| Proptech content teams (developers' in-house) | Budget | Only serve themselves; mid-market developers can't afford them |

**Positioning statement:** *For real estate professionals who live on deal flow, Propel is the marketing department you can't afford to build — vertical-specialist, AI-accelerated, and accountable to leads, not likes.*

### 2.5 Why now

1. AI collapses agency cost structures (2026 video, copy, and automation tooling is production-grade) — first movers in a vertical own the efficiency gap before it becomes table stakes.
2. Diaspora demand and off-plan sales models make video-led remote selling the default, not the exception.
3. The niche is genuinely unconsolidated — no dominant "real estate marketing" brand exists in Nigeria. Category kings get built in exactly these windows.

## 4. Business & Revenue Model

- **Primary:** Monthly retainers (₦250k / ₦600k / ₦1.2m tiers), 3-month minimum term, paid monthly in advance. Target blended average ₦450k/client/month in year 1.
- **Secondary:** Project fees (Propel Launch: ₦1.5m–₦5m per development launch; Propel OS setup: ₦350k–₦900k one-off).
- **Tertiary (year 2+):** Performance kickers on developer mandates (per-unit-sold bonus), productized templates/courses for agents who can't afford retainers, and white-label AI marketing tools.
- **Unit economics target:** ≥60% gross margin per retainer (AI leverage + Nigerian talent costs), CAC recovered within 2 months, 9+ month average client lifetime.
- **Payment reality (Lagos):** invoice in naira, collect via bank transfer/Paystack; require month-in-advance to eliminate receivables risk; price FX-exposed tooling costs into retainers with an annual review clause.

## 5. Operating Model

- **Remote-first:** strategy, content, ads, automation, and reporting run fully remote. No office until revenue demands one; use meeting spaces/co-working day passes for pitches (₦25k–₦50k/day).
- **Physical when it wins deals:** property shoots, developer pitches, NIESV/industry events, and quarterly client dinners. Studio work delivered through a vetted freelance production network (videographers/drone pilots paid per shoot) — zero fixed production payroll.
- **Pod structure at scale:** one pod = strategist (partner initially) + content/AI producer + media buyer serves 8–12 retainer clients. Add pods as revenue grows.
- **AI-forward by design:** every workflow is built AI-first from day one (see [docs/06-ai-stack.md](06-ai-stack.md)). Rule of thumb: if a task is done twice, it gets a prompt template; if done weekly, it gets an automation.

## 6. Legal, Regulatory & Structure

*(Action list — not legal advice; engage a CAC-accredited lawyer/accountant, budget ₦250k–₦400k.)*

1. **Incorporate** a Private Limited Company (Ltd) with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC) — e.g., "Propel Digital Ltd" (check name availability; register 2–3 fallback names). Cost incl. professional fees: ~₦150k–₦250k.
2. **Partnership terms — put in the Shareholders' Agreement before revenue arrives:** equity split, 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff for both partners, roles & decision rights, capital contribution schedule, dividend/salary policy, deadlock resolution, exit/buy-back mechanics, IP assignment to the company.
3. **Tax & compliance:** FIRS TIN registration, VAT registration (7.5% — invoice VAT-inclusive pricing decisions upfront), annual CAC returns, PAYE once staff join. Retain an accountant on a small monthly retainer (~₦50k/mo).
4. **Banking & payments:** corporate account (dual signatory), Paystack/Flutterwave merchant account for card/transfer collection, domiciliary account for any FX-billed clients (diaspora developers are a real segment).
5. **Contracts:** standard Master Services Agreement + per-package Statements of Work; 3-month minimum term, month-in-advance payment, late-payment service-pause clause, content IP licence terms, and a limitation-of-liability clause (we market properties; we do not verify title — this must be explicit).
6. **Data protection:** comply with the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) for lead data we capture on clients' behalf — privacy notice, consent capture on forms, basic data-processing terms in the MSA.
7. **Advertising standards:** ARCON regulates advertising exposure in Nigeria; ensure claims in property ads ("C of O available", ROI promises) are client-warranted in writing.

## 8. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Clients churn when leads dip (commission-income volatility) | High | 3-month minimum terms; month-in-advance billing; report weekly on leading indicators; diversify across segments |
| Late/non-payment culture | High | No work without advance payment; automated service pause; Paystack recurring billing where possible |
| Naira volatility inflating USD tool costs | Medium-High | Annual price review clause; prefer tools with NGN billing or one-time costs; keep tool spend <8% of revenue |
| Fraud-adjacent clients (marketing disputed land) damages brand | Medium | Client vetting checklist (CAC status, NIESV/ESVARBON where applicable, title docs sighted); MSA liability carve-out; walk away publicly if needed |
| Key-person dependence on partners' networks | Medium | Systematize sales (CRM, referral program) from month 1; document all delivery playbooks |
| AI commoditizes our edge as competitors adopt it | Medium (18–24mo) | Our moat migrates from *tools* to *vertical data + brand + systems*: proprietary prompt/creative libraries, performance benchmarks by Lagos micro-market, category-king brand |
| Power/connectivity disrupting remote delivery | Medium | Team equipment stipend covers inverter/Starlink-class connectivity; delivery SLAs with buffer |
| Meta/TikTok ad account bans (common in NG real estate vertical) | Medium | Agency-level ad accounts, strict compliance review of creatives, backup accounts and platform diversification |
